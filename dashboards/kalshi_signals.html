How net edge is read

On a binary contract, the best YES bid and best NO bid should sum near 100¢. When they sum to less, that gap is the gross mispricing. Net edge subtracts both legs' $0.02 fee plus a slippage haircut scaled to how thin the book is. What survives is a real edge — usually a fraction of a cent, if anything.

Why red is the honest default

Liquid markets are efficient. The gap almost always dies once you price in fees and the contracts you could actually clear. A green row is rare and worth a human look — and even then, you place the order. No bot, no keys, no promises.
